Knowledge Hub Second Wave

Editorial Collection

Knowledge Hub Second Wave groups a larger expansion of Electronic Artefacts knowledge records across hypertext, open source, metadata, creative coding, web audio, motion, typography, HCI, algorithmic music and generative AI.

active research

What this entry establishes.

A concise view of its scope, position, limitations and supporting sources.

Collection thesis

Editorial thesis

The second Knowledge Hub wave expands from graph foundations into durable cultural and technical fields that can attract search traffic while strengthening Electronic Artefacts as an open research library.

Selection note

Selection principles

This wave prioritizes evergreen topics with strong search intent, deep internal linking potential and long-term relevance to creative technology, archives and knowledge systems.

Thesis

Knowledge Hub Second Wave extends the first foundation collection with topics that are broad enough to remain useful for decades and specific enough to support organic discovery.

Selection logic

The collection selects articles and entities that can connect multiple disciplines: hypertext, open source, metadata, web audio, creative coding pedagogy, procedural graphics, motion, typography, HCI, algorithmic music and generative AI.

Editorial role

This collection is designed as an onboarding path for readers who arrive through search and then discover Electronic Artefacts projects, programs and research fields through contextual links.

Current members

The wave includes twelve publications, thirteen concept records and five technology records.

Future expansion

Future waves should add methods, tool records, datasets, archive records and project-specific case studies that reuse these entities instead of creating duplicate categories.
