CONCEPT

Personal Knowledge System

A personal knowledge system is a practice and tool environment for capturing, linking, revising and retrieving notes, sources, ideas and decisions over time.

Personal knowledge systems connect digital gardens, wikis, note archives, research methods, backlinks and long-term intellectual memory.

Editorial position

Definition

A personal knowledge system is an ongoing environment for capturing information, linking ideas and turning notes into reusable understanding.

Scope

The concept includes notes, source records, backlinks, tags, outlines, maps of content, spaced review, digital gardens and research logs.

Applications

Electronic Artefacts can adapt this logic at site scale. The Knowledge Hub is not personal, but it benefits from the same practices: stable pages, explicit links, source trails and recurring revision.

Limitations

Capture without synthesis creates clutter. The system becomes valuable when notes are rewritten, linked, pruned and published with context.
