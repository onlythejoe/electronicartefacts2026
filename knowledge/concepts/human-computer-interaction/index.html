CONCEPT

Human Computer Interaction

Human computer interaction is the study and design of relationships between people, computational systems, interfaces, tasks, environments and cultures of use.

HCI connects interface design, accessibility, creative tools, augmented intelligence, software ergonomics and the social consequences of computation.

Editorial claims

Position maintained by Electronic Artefacts

  1. HCI is central to creative tools because interfaces shape what creators can perceive and attempt.
  2. Good interfaces expose state, preserve agency and make recovery possible.

Boundaries

What this concept excludes

  1. Pure visual styling with no relation to interaction, cognition or use
  2. Automation design that ignores human agency and error recovery

Definition

Human computer interaction studies and designs the relationships between people and computational systems.

Scope

The concept includes interface behavior, feedback, mental models, usability, accessibility, error handling, creative flow, automation and social context.

Applications

Electronic Artefacts uses HCI to reason about VASTE, knowledge navigation, graph tools, audio interfaces and AI-assisted creative systems.

Limitations

HCI can become too narrow when reduced to usability metrics alone. Cultural context, power, learning and meaning also matter.

References

See WCAG, Engelbart, augmented intelligence and motion design.
