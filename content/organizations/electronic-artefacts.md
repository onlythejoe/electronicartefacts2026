## Institutional role

Electronic Artefacts designs and builds digital products, knowledge systems and cultural platforms. Client commissions address concrete needs; proprietary programs turn recurring methods into reusable technology; research and artistic publishing create space for questions beyond a commercial brief.

## Operating layers

Research frames the question. Programs retain reusable logic. Projects put that logic to work in public, cultural or client contexts. Publications make the thinking citable. The archive preserves evidence, versions and lineage.

## Public knowledge system

The public platform connects these layers through stable entities and explicit relationships. A project can be traced back to the concepts, methods, programs, people and evidence that made it possible without forcing every visitor to learn the underlying model first.

## Responsibilities

Electronic Artefacts distinguishes publication status, maturity, confidence and visibility. This allows experimental work to remain public without presenting it as proven, finished or available on the same terms as a delivered product.
